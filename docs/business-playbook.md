# TopPicked — Business Playbook

## The Pitch (Elevator Version)

Amazon product listings are broken. Sellers dump thousands into SEO-gaming their titles, buying fake reviews, and paying for sponsored rankings. The average shopper sees 50 identical-looking results and has no idea what's actually good.

**TopPicked is the independent filter.** We buy products, test them, and publish honest reviews. When people buy through our links, we earn 1-10% Amazon Associates commission. Zero inventory, zero shipping, zero customer service.

## Content Strategy: How to Get Traffic

### 1. Target "Best X for Y" Keywords
These are the highest-converting search queries for affiliate content:
- "Best USB-C hub for MacBook"
- "Best kitchen knife under $50"
- "Best desk lamp for home office"

**Each review page targets 1 primary keyword + 3-5 related keywords.**

### 2. Write Comparison Content ("X vs Y")
These rank incredibly well and convert at 2x the rate of standalone reviews:
- "Anker vs Satechi USB-C hub"
- "Ember Mug vs regular mug — worth it?"
- "AirPods Pro vs Sony WF-1000XM5"

### 3. Seasonal Content Calendar
| Month | Content Focus |
|-------|--------------|
| January | New Year health/fitness, home organization |
| February | Valentine's gift guides |
| March–April | Spring cleaning, outdoor gear |
| May | Mother's Day gifts |
| June | Father's Day gifts, summer gear |
| July | Prime Day deals roundup |
| September | Back to school, home office |
| November | Black Friday/Cyber Monday deals |
| December | Holiday gift guides |

### 4. Build Topical Authority
Google rewards sites that go deep on a topic. Pick 3-4 categories to start and publish 10+ reviews in each before expanding.

**Recommended starter categories (high commission, high search volume):**
- Home Office (monitors, keyboards, mice, chairs)
- Kitchen (knives, cookware, coffee gear)
- Tech & Gadgets (cables, chargers, accessories)

## SEO Strategy

### On-Page SEO Checklist (Every Review)
- [ ] Title tag: "Best [Product] Review (2026) — Is It Worth It?"
- [ ] Meta description: 150-160 chars, include "honest review" + key feature
- [ ] H1: Product name + review hook
- [ ] H2s for each section (Our Take, Pros, Cons, Verdict)
- [ ] Image alt tags with product name
- [ ] Internal links to related reviews and category pages
- [ ] Affiliate link uses `rel="nofollow sponsored"` (required by Google)
- [ ] Schema markup (Product, Review structured data)

### Off-Page SEO
- Build backlinks via guest posting on tech/lifestyle blogs
- Get cited in "best of" roundup articles
- Share on Reddit (genuinely helpful, not spammy) in relevant subreddits
- Answer Quora questions with links to your reviews

### Technical SEO
- Server-side rendering (Next.js SSR/SSG) — Google loves this
- Fast page load (Vercel CDN, optimized images)
- Mobile-responsive design
- XML sitemap auto-generated by Next.js
- Clean URL structure: `/product/product-name`, `/category/category-name`

## UI/UX Best Practices (from the skills pack)

These are the design principles that make affiliate sites convert:

### Trust Signals
- **"Never Sponsored" badge** in hero — establishes independence immediately
- **Real ratings alongside Amazon ratings** — shows you have your own opinion
- **Transparent affiliate disclosure** — legally required AND builds trust
- **Review methodology page** — explains how you test

### Product Page Optimization
- **Large, high-quality product images** on white background
- **Price prominently displayed** — people need to see the number fast
- **"Buy on Amazon" button in Amazon orange (#FF9900)** — universally recognized
- **Pros/cons in visual cards** — scannable, not walls of text
- **Bottom CTA after full review** — catch people who scrolled the whole thing
- **Multiple CTAs per page** — top, middle (after pros/cons), bottom

### Mobile Optimization (60%+ of traffic is mobile)
- Touch-friendly button sizes (min 44px height)
- Single-column layout on mobile
- Sticky "Buy on Amazon" bar on scroll
- Fast image loading (lazy load + WebP)

## Growth Automation (Self-Improving System)

The business should get better over time without constant manual work. Here's how:

### Automated Workflows

1. **Product Discovery** → Find trending/new products in your categories
2. **Review Draft Generation** → AI generates first draft from Amazon data
3. **Price Tracking** → Monitor price drops for deals page
4. **SEO Monitoring** → Track keyword rankings, identify improvements
5. **Click Analytics** → Know which products convert, write more like those
6. **Content Expansion** → AI suggests new review topics based on search trends

### Self-Annealing Loop
```
1. Publish a review
2. Track: clicks, conversions, SEO ranking, bounce rate
3. Identify what works: which reviews get clicks? which keywords rank?
4. Do more of what works, less of what doesn't
5. System improves over time
```

### Automation Scripts (see `directives/` and `execution/`)
- `auto-improve.md` — Directive for continuous improvement cycle
- `product-research.md` — Directive for finding new products to review
- `content-repurposer.md` — Turn reviews into social media posts
- `seo-optimization.md` — Automated SEO analysis and improvement
